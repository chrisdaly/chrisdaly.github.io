Over the past few decades football has been criticised by many for being too predictable. The elite clubs in europe continue to increase their revenues year after year and thus can spend small fortunes on the best players of smaller clubs. This drastically reduces the competitveness of each domestic league making it less unpredictable and interesting to watch.

In information theory entropy is a measure of information and uncertainty. This analysis utilizes entropy as a measurement of the unpredictabiity of each league. It is computed based on the probabilities of each outcome for every game based on the odds from bookmakers. This measurement is averaged for each team and season.

Entropy is at its minimum when one outcome has a probability of 1 (H = 0, totally predictable).
Entropy is at its maximum when each outcome has an equal probability (H = 1.098, totally unpredictable).
